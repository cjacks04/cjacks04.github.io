I am an Assistant Professor at the University of Wisconsin–Madison [Information School](https://ischool.wisc.edu/), where I study how people engage with data- and AI-driven systems in public and community settings.

My research sits at the intersection of human-computer interaction, computer-supported cooperative work, and social computing. I focus on responsible AI, civic technology, environmental justice, participatory design, and human–AI collaboration.

I use mixed methods to study how technical systems shape participation, expertise, and decision-making, especially in public and institutional settings. Across my work, I am interested in how non-experts make sense of complex systems, how community knowledge can inform design and governance, and how computing can better support public life.

I hold a Ph.D. in Information Science and Technology from Syracuse University, an M.S. in Library and Information Science from the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign, and a B.A. in Political Science from the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign.

My research has been supported by the National Science Foundation, the Rockefeller Foundation, the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, and the UW–Madison Office of the Vice Chancellor for Research and Graduate Education.
