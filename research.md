  <div class="research-intro">
    <h2>Research</h2>
    <p>
      My research examines how people learn, participate, and exercise judgment in data-rich and AI-mediated environments.
      Across projects, I focus on how expertise is developed, how human and machine intelligence interact, and how data-driven
      systems can be designed to better reflect community values and public needs.
    </p>
  </div>

  <!-- ========================= -->
  <!-- AUGMENTED EXPERTISE -->
  <!-- ========================= -->

  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Augmented Expertise</h3>

    <p class="research-question">
      How can we cultivate specialized expertise among distributed, non-expert participants through adaptive scaffolding and hybrid human–AI systems?
    </p>

    <p>
      This research explores how people develop domain-specific expertise in complex environments without formal training.
      Drawing on work in citizen science systems such as Gravity Spy, I examine how interfaces, feedback, and structured participation
      enable non-experts to engage in sophisticated analytical work and contribute to scientific discovery.
    </p>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Example Projects</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Crowdsourced learning environments</li>
        <li>Language socialization in online communities</li>
        <li>Motivation and participation in citizen science</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Contributions</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Demonstrates how expertise emerges through participation rather than formal training</li>
        <li>Introduces language adoption as a measurable mechanism of socialization and learning</li>
        <li>Shows how structured task design and feedback loops support skill development in distributed systems</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Representative Work</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Jackson, C. (ICWSM). Socialization through language adoption in virtual citizen science</li>
        <li>Jackson et al. (2020). Machine learning-guided training in citizen science (Computers in Human Behavior)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- HYBRID INTELLIGENCE -->
  <!-- ========================= -->

  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Hybrid Intelligence Systems</h3>

    <p class="research-question">
      How can humans and AI systems learn together in ways that improve both human capability and machine performance?
    </p>

    <p>
      This work moves beyond traditional pipelines where humans simply label data for AI systems.
      Instead, I study co-learning environments where humans and algorithms iteratively improve one another,
      forming adaptive systems that evolve over time.
    </p>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Example Projects</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Gravity Spy and machine learning integration</li>
        <li>AI-assisted classification systems</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Contributions</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Introduces co-learning as a model for human–AI interaction</li>
        <li>Shows how humans can act as more than data labelers—serving as analytical collaborators</li>
        <li>Demonstrates how hybrid systems improve both model performance and human expertise</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Representative Work</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Gravity Spy: Lessons learned and a path forward</li>
        <li>Supporting human and machine co-learning in citizen science</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Algorithmic and Data Justice</h3>

    <p class="research-question">
      How can data-driven systems in the public sector operate equitably and reflect community values?
    </p>

    <p>
      This research examines how AI systems embed assumptions about fairness, legitimacy, and decision-making.
      It develops participatory approaches in which communities act as evaluators of algorithmic systems,
      bringing contextual knowledge and normative reasoning into oversight processes.
    </p>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Example Projects</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Participatory AI auditing</li>
        <li>Public perceptions of data use</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Contributions</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Reframes AI auditing as a participatory and socially grounded process</li>
        <li>Demonstrates the importance of normative reasoning in evaluating algorithmic systems</li>
        <li>Bridges technical AI evaluation with community-centered perspectives on fairness</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Representative Work</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Beyond Bias Detection: Community auditors and normative reasoning in AI oversight</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Civic Data and Participation</h3>

    <p class="research-question">
      How can communities meaningfully participate in data-driven governance and decision-making?
    </p>

    <p>
      This work focuses on inequities in access to data and participation in civic processes.
      I study how environmental and public-sector data systems can better represent community knowledge,
      particularly in contexts of environmental justice and local decision-making.
    </p>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Example Projects</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Environmental justice mapping</li>
        <li>Civic data representativeness</li>
        <li>Knowledge Map (community data portal)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Contributions</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Identifies structural barriers to participation in civic data systems</li>
        <li>Develops models for understanding representativeness in community-generated data</li>
        <li>Designs systems that integrate community narratives with institutional data</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="research-section">
      <strong>Representative Work</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>Seeing Like a Community: Public perceptions of data use in government (CHI 2026)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
